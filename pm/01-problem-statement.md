# Problem Statement

Vanguard's long-term mutual fund investors — particularly retirees and near-retirees managing balanced portfolios across multiple stock and bond funds — face a structurally complex decision every time they need to make a portfolio withdrawal. Because these investors have accumulated shares over many years through contributions, reinvestments, and partial drawdowns, their cost basis histories are layered and fund-specific, creating meaningful variation in the capital gains consequences of selling from one fund versus another. At the same time, any withdrawal that draws unevenly from the portfolio risks pushing asset allocations out of balance, potentially increasing long-term risk exposure. Today, Vanguard's investor portal treats these as separate concerns: portfolio allocation tools ignore tax basis entirely, while cost basis and gain/loss reporting tools offer no withdrawal guidance and no connection to rebalancing logic. The result is that investors must either make uninformed decisions, rely on manual spreadsheet analysis, or pay for outside financial advice to answer what should be a solvable, data-driven question: *given my withdrawal need, which funds should I sell, in what amounts, to keep my portfolio balanced while minimizing my tax burden?* The absence of an integrated, guided withdrawal tool leaves a high-value user segment underserved at precisely the moment their need — and their anxiety — is highest.
