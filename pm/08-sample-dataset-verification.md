## Sample Data Coverage Map

*Maps each of the 15 coverage cases from 07b-logical-data-model.md to the specific dataset element that exercises it.*

---

**Case 1 — Baseline moderate complexity, no edge conditions**
Satisfied by: The portfolio's core structure — three accounts, six funds, a defined target allocation, YTD gains in both ST and LT categories, no missing data at session initiation. Any session in which the user enters a withdrawal amount below the 10% threshold ($84,985), with no Wait & Save lots in the selected funds and no RMD shortfall, exercises the baseline path. Recommended test amount: $25,000 from VTSAX taxable only.

---

**Case 2 — High portfolio complexity**
Satisfied by: The full portfolio — 3 accounts, 6 distinct fund holdings, 32 total lots (9 VTSAX lots alone spanning 2004–2025), layered cost basis histories, mix of ST and LT lots across all funds, and significant allocation drift. This exercises the Workflow B data table at high complexity and validates performance against REQ-NF-PERF-002 and REQ-NF-PERF-004.

---

**Case 3 — No target allocation on record**
Satisfied by: Removing or nulling the `target_allocation` object in the dataset. The tool must degrade gracefully to tax-only optimization mode per PM Decision 7, display a prominent notice, and provide inline access to the simplified allocation editor defaulting to 50/50. This case is a dataset modification test, not a dataset state test.

---

**Case 4 — RMD-affected account present**
Satisfied by: `rmd_records[0]` — Traditional IRA (ACCT-TRAD-IRA-001) with $7,867.92 RMD required for 2026, $4,200.00 distributed YTD, $3,667.92 remaining. The RMD notice must display at Step A3 and be carried forward as informational-only throughout the session.

---

**Case 5 — Wait & Save condition present**
Satisfied by: Lot `T-VTIAX-07` — 400 shares of VTIAX acquired April 25, 2025, converting to LT in 14 days (April 25, 2026). Estimated tax savings by waiting: $55.80. This is the primary Wait & Save trigger. The interrupt should fire when this lot is selected or encountered in Workflow B Step B2.

---

**Case 6 — Tax-optimal recommendation produces meaningful allocation drift**
Satisfied by: The portfolio's allocation state (domestic equity +2.77% overweight, international equity +1.41% overweight, bonds -4.11% underweight). A tax-optimal recommendation will likely favor selling from VBIRX or VBTLX (taxable) to harvest losses, even though bonds are already underweight — this worsens the allocation imbalance. The correct recommendation to improve balance would sell from overweight equities (VTSAX or VTIAX), but these carry substantial unrealized gains. This tension exercises the drift notice requirement (REQ-A4-006) and the balance-priority alternative (REQ-A4-007, PM Decision 6).

---

**Case 7 — Harvestable loss present**
Satisfied by: Five lots across two funds in the taxable account — T-VBIRX-02 (-$575.00 LT), T-VBIRX-03 (-$540.00 LT), T-VBTLX-01 (-$3,030.00 LT), T-VBTLX-02 (-$4,540.00 LT), T-VBTLX-03 (-$705.00 LT). Total harvestable loss in taxable account: -$9,390.00. The optimization engine (REQ-OE-004) and the Workflow B visual indicator (REQ-B2-002) must both surface these opportunities.

---

**Case 8 — Large relative withdrawal (exceeds 10% of portfolio)**
Satisfied by: Any withdrawal amount above $84,985.14 entered at Step A2. Recommended test amounts: $90,000, $95,000, $100,000. The soft informational notice (REQ-A2-003) must display without blocking progression. At $100,000, the withdrawal represents 11.77% of the $849,851.40 portfolio — meaningfully above the threshold and large enough to materially affect fund balances and allocation post-sale.

---

**Case 9 — YTD gains data unavailable**
Satisfied by: Simulating a retrieval failure for the `ytd_gains_record` object. The tool must surface a specific notice that YTD data is unavailable (REQ-NF-DATA-005), proceed without the YTD summary, and not display any tax impact projection that depends on YTD figures without disclosure. This is a system behavior test requiring backend simulation of a data retrieval failure.

---

**Case 10 — Returning user with prior incomplete session**
Satisfied by: Modifying `user.prior_session` to: `session_status: "incomplete"`, `scenario_saved: true`, `session_duration_minutes: 12`. The deep-link entry control must display as "Continue where you left off" and land at Step A2 with prior session context surfaced. Session duration (12 minutes) exceeds the 5-minute suppression threshold and a scenario was saved, so the deep-link must not be suppressed.

---

**Case 11 — Returning user below deep-link suppression threshold**
Satisfied by: Modifying `user.prior_session` to: `session_duration_minutes: 4`, `scenario_saved: false`. Both suppression conditions are met (under 5 minutes AND no scenario saved). The deep-link must be suppressed and the full tool entry screen displayed instead per REQ-G-029 and PM Decision 9.

---

**Case 12 — Three-scenario comparison**
Satisfied by: The full portfolio — sufficient funds and lot variety to support generating one Workflow A recommendation and two meaningfully different Workflow B configurations (e.g., Scenario 1: tax-optimal from Workflow A; Scenario 2: balance-prioritized manual configuration selling from VTSAX and VTIAX; Scenario 3: loss-harvesting configuration selling VBIRX and VBTLX). All three scenarios will show materially different tax impact and allocation outcomes given the portfolio's current drift and harvestable losses.

---

**Case 13 — Workflow B entry without prior Workflow A recommendation**
Satisfied by: Navigating directly from the tool entry screen (Step A1) to Workflow B without passing through Steps A2–A4. All sell amount fields must be blank on entry. The "Return to system recommendation" control must not display per REQ-B1-005a. The user must be able to build and save a scenario without a Workflow A starting point.

---

**Case 14 — Non-default tax bracket selected**
Satisfied by: Opening the rate table at Step A3 and selecting any bracket other than the default mid-range (24% ST / 15% LT). With $9,995.00 in YTD gains already realized and significant unrealized gains across the portfolio, bracket changes produce meaningfully different projected tax liability figures. Recommended test brackets: lowest (0% LT rate) and highest (20% LT rate + 3.8% NIIT) to exercise the full range of recalculation behavior across all scenarios and the recommendation.

---

**Case 15 — Accounting method change mid-session in Workflow B**
Satisfied by: VTSAX (9 lots spanning 2004–2025) and VBTLX taxable (4 lots including harvestable loss lots). For VTSAX, FIFO would sell the 2004 lot first (lowest cost basis, highest gain), while specific lot identification could target the 2025 lots (lowest unrealized gain). The difference in projected tax impact is substantial: FIFO realizes approximately $22,000 in gains on the first 200 shares sold; specific identification on the two most recent lots realizes approximately $4,724. Changing the accounting method mid-session for VTSAX must trigger immediate recalculation per REQ-B3-003 and exercise the constraint validation in REQ-OE-005.
