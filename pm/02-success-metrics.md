# Success Metrics

## User Behavior Metrics

**1. Tool Adoption Rate**
Percentage of eligible users (those with multi-fund portfolios and at least one withdrawal in the past 12 months) who use the Sell & Rebalance tool at least once within 90 days of launch. Target: 20% in year one.

**2. Workflow Completion Rate**
Percentage of sessions in which a user who enters the tool proceeds through to the Scenario Comparison view. Measures whether the tool is navigable and trustworthy enough to sustain engagement through to a decision point. Target: 65% of initiated sessions.

**3. Recommendation Acceptance Rate (Workflow A)**
Percentage of system-generated recommendations that users execute without modification, or with only minor manual adjustments before executing. A high rate signals that the AI rationale is legible and trusted; a very high rate may warrant investigation for over-reliance. Target: 40–60% accepted as-is.

**4. Return Usage Rate**
Percentage of users who use the tool more than once within a 12-month period. Indicates whether the tool earns a place in users' ongoing withdrawal routine rather than being a one-time curiosity. Target: 50% of first-time users return within 12 months.

**5. Scenario Comparison Utilization**
Percentage of completed sessions in which the user creates and views at least 2 scenarios before executing. Indicates whether the comparison feature is genuinely supporting decision-making rather than being bypassed. Target: 35% of completing sessions.

## Business Metrics

**6. Assets Under Management Retention**
Change in 12-month AUM attrition rate among tool users versus a matched control group of eligible non-users. The hypothesis is that users who feel supported in withdrawal decisions are less likely to transfer assets to a competitor or advisor. Target: measurable reduction in attrition among tool users.

**7. Advisor Escalation Reduction**
Change in the rate at which tool-eligible users contact Vanguard Personal Advisor Services or call-center support with withdrawal-related questions, in the period following tool launch. A meaningful reduction would indicate the tool is resolving a category of need that previously required human intervention. Target: 15% reduction in withdrawal-related support contacts among eligible users within 12 months.

---

*Note: All targets above are proposed starting benchmarks and should be validated against historical engagement data and comparable Vanguard tool launches prior to committing them to a product scorecard.*
